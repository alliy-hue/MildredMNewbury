About Mildred

Thoughtful, relationship‑based book marketing rooted in real reading communities.

I didn’t start in a big agency or publishing house. I started with a simple love of books and conversation. For years, I was the friend who always had a book to recommend—and a book club to match it with.

Over time, I noticed a pattern: some books exploded in conversation once the right readers found them. Others quietly disappeared, not because they weren’t good, but because they never reached the people who would truly appreciate them. Authors began asking how I was matching books with groups so well—and whether I could help them do it on purpose.

That curiosity became my work. Today, I design and manage thoughtful, community‑based campaigns that help authors escape random tactics. We focus on clarity, fit, and respect—for the story and the reader.

What I believe about book marketing

  • A book is more than a product; it’s a piece of someone’s heart and mind.
  • Readers are more than numbers; they are people with tastes, histories, and lives.
  • Visibility isn’t about shouting—it’s about being in the right places, at the right time.
  • Integrity matters. Shortcuts that damage trust are never worth it.

Authors I work especially well with

  • Debut authors who want a calm, guided launch instead of chaos and guesswork.
  • Indie or small‑press authors who feel their books never got the attention they deserved.
  • Thoughtful, often introverted authors who value genuine connection over hype.
  • Authors with a quiet backlist who want a second life for the stories they still believe in.
